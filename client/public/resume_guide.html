Section 01

7 MISTAKES
KILLING YOUR RESUME

These are the exact reasons 9 out of 10 Indian resumes never reach a human recruiter. Check each one against your own resume right now.

01

❌ MULTI-COLUMN TEMPLATES THAT ATS CANNOT READ

The beautiful two-column template you downloaded from Canva or Pinterest is invisible to ATS software. When the parser encounters multiple columns, it either reads them out of order — mixing your job titles with your skills — or skips entire sections. The result: your carefully crafted resume becomes meaningless jumbled text. Always use a single-column layout for digital applications. Save the fancy design for a portfolio website.

02

❌ SENDING THE SAME RESUME TO EVERY JOB

Sending one generic resume to 300 companies is the single biggest resume mistake Indian job seekers make. Every ATS scores your resume based on keyword match percentage with the specific job description. A resume with a 55% match is auto-rejected instantly. A 78% match moves forward. This difference is just a matter of adding and reordering the right keywords — which takes 10 minutes per application and multiplies your callback rate by 3x or more.

03

❌ VAGUE BULLETS WITH NO NUMBERS OR IMPACT

"Responsible for managing a team" tells a recruiter absolutely nothing. Compare that to: "Led a 7-member cross-functional team to ship a real-time payments feature 3 weeks ahead of schedule, reducing transaction failure rate by 28%." The second version is memorable, credible, and passes ATS keyword checks. Every single bullet point on your resume should describe an action you took and a result you achieved — with a number whenever possible.

04

❌ INCLUDING PHOTO, DOB, AND PERSONAL DETAILS

Including your photograph, date of birth, religion, caste, or marital status on a resume is outdated and actively harmful to your chances at MNCs, product companies, and modern Indian startups. Many ATS systems cannot parse these sections and skip surrounding content as a result. Modern hiring guidelines at companies like Google India, Microsoft India, Flipkart, and Razorpay explicitly discourage this information. Keep contact details to: name, phone, professional email, LinkedIn URL, GitHub (tech roles), and city.

05

❌ UNPROFESSIONAL EMAIL ADDRESS

Your email address is the very first thing a recruiter sees at the top of your resume. Addresses like "coolboy_1999@gmail.com," "princess_shreya@yahoo.com," or "rockstar_coder@rediffmail.com" signal immaturity instantly. Create a new professional email — ideally firstname.lastname@gmail.com — before applying to any job. It takes 3 minutes and is one of the easiest credibility upgrades you can make to your resume immediately.

06

❌ PASSIVE LANGUAGE AND WEAK VERBS

Starting bullet points with passive phrases like "Was responsible for," "Helped with," "Assisted in," or "Worked on" signals low ownership and weak contribution. Replace every passive phrase with a strong action verb: Engineered, Architected, Drove, Launched, Reduced, Increased, Negotiated, Secured, Designed, Delivered, Optimised. The verb you choose signals how much ownership you took of your work — recruiters notice immediately.

07

❌ SKILLS LISTED WITHOUT PROOF OR CONTEXT

A flat list — "Skills: Python, Machine Learning, SQL, Leadership, Communication" — scores very low with both ATS and human reviewers. ATS checks whether your listed skills appear in your experience section too. Human reviewers look for evidence. If you claim Python, there should be a project or role where you used it. If you claim leadership, there should be a bullet describing a team you led. Every skill needs a shadow — somewhere in your resume that backs it up.

Section 02

HOW ATS WORKS
AT INDIAN COMPANIES

Understanding what happens the second you click "Apply" is your biggest competitive advantage. Most candidates have no idea this filter even exists.

What Happens When You Click "Apply"

The moment you submit your resume on Naukri, LinkedIn, or a company's careers page, it enters an Applicant Tracking System (ATS) — software that reads, parses, and scores your resume automatically before any human sees it. Companies like Infosys, HCL, Wipro, Accenture India, Flipkart, Swiggy, Zomato, and virtually every company hiring at scale uses one of these systems: Taleo, Workday, Greenhouse, iCIMS, or their own proprietary platform.

The ATS extracts your name, contact information, education, work experience, and skills — then scores your resume based on how well it matches the job description. Resumes that score above approximately 70–75% proceed to the recruiter's inbox. Everything below is auto-rejected, usually with no notification to you.

This is why you can apply to 80 jobs and receive zero responses — not because you're unqualified, but because your resume's formatting or keywords don't pass the ATS filter. ResumeLens replicates this scoring process so you can see your exact score before you apply.

💡 THE KEYWORD TRICK

Open the job description. Copy and paste it into a word counter. Find the words that appear most frequently. These are your target keywords. Add every relevant one to your resume — in your summary, experience bullets, and skills section. This single change can move your ATS score from 55% to 80%.

ATS Rules That Every Indian Job Seeker Must Follow

Use Exact Phrases from the JD

If the job description says "cross-functional collaboration," use that exact phrase. If it says "Agile methodology," don't write "Scrum-based development." ATS systems do exact and semantic matching — the closer your language to the JD, the higher your score.

Standard Section Headings Only

Use "Work Experience," "Education," "Skills," "Projects," "Certifications." Never use creative alternatives like "My Journey," "Where I've Worked," or "Things I Know." ATS systems look for standard labels and skip sections they don't recognise.

Never Use Headers/Footers for Key Info

Older ATS systems (still common at large Indian IT firms) cannot read content in Word document headers and footers. Your name and contact info must be in the main body of the document — not in a header field.

Save as PDF Unless Told Otherwise

PDF preserves formatting and works with most modern ATS. Some older systems prefer .docx — check the application portal's requirements. When in doubt, PDF is the safer choice for Indian job applications in 2025.

No Graphics, Tables, or Text Boxes

Every design element that "looks nice" is a parsing risk. ATS systems read plain text. A table containing your skills section may be parsed as empty. An icon next to your phone number may cause the number to be missed. Keep it text-only.

THE CORRECT SECTION ORDER

1. Contact Information

Full name (large, bold), phone number with country code, professional Gmail address, LinkedIn profile URL, GitHub link (for tech roles only), city and state. No photo. No date of birth. No full home address — city is sufficient.

2. Professional Summary (3–4 Lines)

Your elevator pitch. Who you are, how many years of experience, what your core skills are, and what role you're targeting. Freshers: use "Objective Statement" instead. Never write "Seeking a challenging role in a dynamic organization" — it means nothing. Be specific: mention the domain, the skill, and the type of company.

3. Work Experience (Reverse Chronological)

Most recent job first. For each role: Company name, Your job title, Employment dates (Month Year – Month Year or Present), and 3–5 bullet points. Each bullet must start with an action verb and include a quantified outcome where possible. This section is the most important part of your resume — spend 70% of your writing effort here.

4. Education

Freshers: place this section above Work Experience. Experienced professionals: place it after. Include degree name, institution, graduation year, and CGPA (only if 7.5+ on a 10-point scale). You can remove 10th and 12th details once you have 2+ years of work experience. Mention any academic honours or rank if applicable.

5. Skills

Group into subcategories for tech roles: Programming Languages, Frameworks & Libraries, Cloud & DevOps, Databases, Tools. For non-tech roles: Core Competencies, Technical Tools, Soft Skills. Match your listed skills to the job description's requirements. Never list a skill you can't back up with experience or a project.

6. Projects (Critical for Freshers)

List 2–4 significant projects. Format each as: Project Name | Technologies Used | Brief description with your specific role | Quantified outcome if available | GitHub link. Projects are your proof of skill when you lack professional experience — treat them as seriously as work experience entries.

7. Certifications & Achievements

List relevant certifications with the issuing body and year: AWS Certified Solutions Architect (Amazon, 2024), Google Data Analytics Certificate (Coursera, 2024), NPTEL Python Programming (IIT Madras, 2023). For achievements: hackathon wins, research publications, patents, sports medals, academic ranks — anything that demonstrates exceptional performance.

Section 04

JOB PORTAL
STRATEGIES FOR INDIA

Each platform works differently. Optimising your profile for each one separately dramatically increases how often recruiters find and contact you.

💼
Naukri.com

India's largest job portal rewards profile completeness and freshness. Update your profile every 7–10 days — even a minor edit resets your "Last Updated" timestamp and pushes you to the top of recruiter searches. Fill in every field: headline, summary, skills, current role, expected salary, preferred locations. Use the keyword-rich "Profile Summary" section as your SEO text — recruiters search by keywords, not just job title. Upload your resume as a separate PDF in addition to your profile. Check your Naukri Resume Score regularly and aim for above 85%.

🔗
LinkedIn India

Turn on "Open to Work" in private mode so only recruiters see it — it won't show the green badge to your current employer. Write a rich "About" section using first person — this is fully indexed and acts as your cover letter. Add at least 5 skills with 10+ endorsements each. Get 3+ recommendations from managers or colleagues. Post original content about your field once a week — LinkedIn's algorithm surfaces active users to recruiters disproportionately. Use a professional headshot with a clean background. Complete your profile to 100% — LinkedIn calls this "All-Star" status.

🎓
Internshala

India's top platform for students, freshers, and candidates seeking internships or entry-level positions. Completeness is rewarded heavily — fill every profile section including your projects, certifications, and extracurriculars. The single biggest differentiator on Internshala is the cover letter — most applicants skip it entirely. Write a personalised 3-paragraph cover letter for every application: why this role, why this company, what specific value you bring. This alone puts you in the top 5% of applicants.

🚀
AngelList / Wellfound

The go-to platform for startup jobs in India. Investors and founders browse profiles directly here. Be explicit about startup experience — if you've demonstrated ownership, built something from scratch, or worn multiple hats, say so in plain language. Include your equity expectations, preferred company stage (seed, Series A, Series B), and desired work style (remote, hybrid, in-office). Link your GitHub, portfolio, or any side projects — startup hiring managers prioritise demonstrated work over credentials and pedigree every time.

🏢
Company Career Pages

The most underutilised channel in India. Top companies — Razorpay, CRED, Groww, Meesho, PhonePe, Delhivery — hire primarily through their own careers pages and give preference to direct applicants over portal submissions. Bookmark the careers page of your top 10 target companies. Set up email alerts if they offer them. When applying directly, customise your resume specifically for that company's tech stack, products, and culture. A well-crafted direct application frequently outperforms 100 portal submissions.

✨
Shine / Monster India

Popular in BFSI, retail, healthcare, manufacturing, and tier-2 cities. Keep your resume marked as "Actively Looking" and update it weekly. Set up job alerts with specific keyword, location, and salary filters — these platforms have large recruiter bases that actively use search. Shine.com has a particularly strong presence for non-tech and blue-collar hiring in India. Monster India connects well with mid-size companies that aren't on Naukri's premium plans. Both platforms work best for roles in traditional industries outside the tech startup ecosystem.

Section 05

THE FRESHER
RESUME PLAYBOOK

Zero work experience doesn't mean a zero-value resume. Here's how thousands of Indian graduates get shortlisted every month — without a single full-time job on their CV.

🏗️

LEAD WITH PROJECTS, NOT EXPERIENCE

Your academic projects, hackathon submissions, freelance work, GitHub repositories, and personal side projects are your experience. Give each one a dedicated entry with: project name, tech stack used, your specific contribution (not the team's), and a measurable outcome (accuracy %, users served, time saved). Treat projects with the same seriousness as job roles. A strong project section beats a weak internship every time.

📜

CERTIFICATIONS CLOSE THE EXPERIENCE GAP

An AWS Cloud Practitioner certificate, Google Data Analytics Professional Certificate, Coursera Python for Everybody, Meta Front-End Developer Certificate, or an NPTEL course from an IIT signals self-motivation and current knowledge. Certifications are especially powerful when you're applying to roles that technically require experience you don't have. They show you can learn independently — a trait every startup and modern company values highly.

🎯

WRITE A TARGETED OBJECTIVE STATEMENT

Never write: "Seeking a challenging role in a dynamic organization that allows me to grow professionally." That is the most cliché line in Indian resume history and signals zero effort. Instead: "Computer Science graduate from NIT Trichy specialising in NLP and Python, targeting an ML Engineer role at a B2B SaaS company in Hyderabad where I can apply my two published research projects on sentiment analysis." Specific. Credible. Memorable.

📊

CGPA: INCLUDE IT OR HIDE IT?

Include your CGPA if it's 7.5 or above on a 10-point scale (3.5+ on a 4.0 scale). Mass hiring at TCS, Infosys, Wipro, Capgemini and Cognizant has CGPA cutoffs — typically 6.0 or 7.0. Product companies and startups care less about CGPA and more about projects and skills. If your CGPA is below 6.5, do not include it — focus energy on making every other section of your resume exceptional to compensate.

FAQ

EVERY QUESTION
INDIAN JOB SEEKERS ASK

Straight answers to the resume questions that matter most in the Indian job market.

Should I include my 10th and 12th marks on my Indian resume?
Yes — if you are a fresher with 0–2 years of experience, include both. Many large IT companies (TCS, Infosys, Wipro, Capgemini) have published cutoffs of 60% or 70% for all three: 10th, 12th, and graduation. Once you have 3+ years of professional experience, drop the 10th and 12th details and list only your highest degree. Product startups and MNCs rarely care about school marks for candidates with real work history.
How important is a cover letter for jobs in India?
For mass hiring via Naukri and Monster, cover letters are rarely read. For startup jobs, senior roles, direct company applications, and platforms like Internshala — cover letters are a real differentiator. A targeted, specific 3-paragraph cover letter that shows you understand the company's product and how your experience solves their exact problem can move you from the bottom to the top of a shortlist instantly. Always have one ready.
Is a 1–2 year employment gap a dealbreaker in India?
No — but you must explain it proactively. Employment gaps are normalised post-COVID. If you used the time to upskill, freelance, or complete certifications, say so directly in your resume summary: "Following a career break focused on AWS certification and a personal ML project in agri-tech." What recruiters dislike is an unexplained gap — not the gap itself. Never lie about employment dates — background verification is standard practice at almost all Indian companies today.
What's the difference between a resume and a CV in India?
In everyday Indian corporate usage, the terms are used interchangeably — don't worry about the distinction for most jobs. Technically, a CV (Curriculum Vitae) is a comprehensive multi-page document covering all academic history, publications, research, and awards. A resume is a concise 1–2 page tailored document for a specific role. CVs are standard in academia, research, medicine, and government interviews. For all corporate, tech, and startup applications: always use a resume.
Should I mention my expected salary on my resume?
Never include salary figures on your resume document itself. On job portals like Naukri, you'll fill separate fields for Current CTC and Expected CTC — that's fine and expected. But your resume should focus entirely on your value, skills, and achievements. Mentioning salary on the resume document either undersells you (if the company's budget was higher) or flags you as too expensive before they've seen your full value. Keep salary for the negotiation conversation.
Can I use AI to write my resume? Will recruiters know?
AI tools are legitimate aids for resume writing — but they work best as editors, not ghostwriters. Use AI to improve phrasing, suggest action verbs, identify keyword gaps, and restructure bullet points. Never let AI fabricate achievements, role titles, or companies you didn't work for. Indian HR professionals in 2025 are increasingly able to recognise over-polished, generic AI text. The winning formula is: your authentic experience and achievements + AI polish and optimisation + ResumeLens ATS verification.
Which companies in India use ATS, and which don't?
Every large Indian company and MNC uses ATS: TCS, Infosys, Wipro, HCL, Accenture India, IBM India, Capgemini, Flipkart, Amazon India, Google India, Microsoft India, Swiggy, Zomato, Ola, PhonePe, Razorpay, HDFC Bank, ICICI, and virtually all companies hiring at scale. Very small startups (under 20 people), family businesses, and hyperlocal companies may not use ATS — but even many Indian seed-funded startups use basic ATS tools through their hiring software. Assume every application goes through some form of automated screening.
